# ABMS Consulting — Design Direction (chosen + alternates)

## Chosen direction: "Institutional Editorial — ABMS Red as the Single Statement"

Probability of occurrence in current AI-generated portfolios: ~0.05 (intentionally rare).

This direction blends Control Risks layout discipline (~50%), BSI Group's accreditation-badge culture (~30%), and Tricor's Asian regional anchoring (~20%), while letting the existing ABMS red carry the only chromatic statement in the system. The rest of the palette stays in institutional black, charcoal, and bone, so that whenever the brand red appears it lands with weight rather than sitting as decoration.

The composition philosophy is editorial and asymmetric. Pages are structured around a left-aligned editorial column with citation-style metadata to the right, hairline rules separating sections instead of cards or boxes, and full-bleed Malaysian institutional photography acting as cinematic plates rather than decorative backgrounds. Numerical and standards data — ISO 37001:2016, Section 17A MACC Act 2009, the countdown digits, and document references — are set in a monospace face to perform a citation function rather than a marketing one. Animation is restrained: short opacity reveals with small upward translations, snap-fast button presses, and no decorative motion.

Color philosophy treats `#B02828` (the extracted ABMS red) as a discipline rather than a palette member. It appears only on the wordmark frame, the primary call-to-action, the accent rule under section openers, the active-state indicator on navigation, and a single stat numeral per section. Secondary accents do not exist; the system intentionally refuses the "burgundy + gold" consulting cliché.

Layout paradigm rejects centered hero columns. The homepage hero anchors text to a left vertical column with negative space across the right two-thirds revealing institutional architecture. The practitioner section uses a 5/7 split with portrait imagery on the left and a long editorial paragraph plus credential ledger on the right. Practice areas are presented as a numbered editorial index (with the index hidden visually behind the headline weight) rather than as audience-routing cards. The training calendar reads as a record of upcoming sessions, like a printed schedule, rather than a marketing list.

Signature elements include: a thin red rule that anchors every section opener, a small-caps monospace eyebrow that sets the section reference (e.g., `§ ANTI-BRIBERY · ISO 37001:2016`), and a footer "colophon" that itemises accreditations, registrations, and PDPA notice the way an annual report does — not the way a SaaS footer does.

Interaction philosophy is quiet. Hover states are restrained to a faint underline emerging from the red rule, focus states are visibly thick (institutional accessibility), and the only motion users will notice is a subtle 180ms opacity reveal when sections enter view. The G7 page adds one operational element — a live countdown to 1 January 2027 — which performs as a typographic clock rather than a marketing badge.

Typography pairs Fraunces (semi-bold to black) for institutional display and editorial section openers, Inter Tight (regular to medium) for body and navigation, and JetBrains Mono for any number, standard reference, or date. The pairing reads as archive-grade rather than agency-grade.

## Alternate direction A — "Compliance Quarterly" (probability ~0.04)

A more aggressively magazine-styled approach modelled after the Financial Times' weekend supplement and BSI's printed standards covers. Drops navigation into a thin upper rule, runs a horizontal red banner across the hero with the firm name set in a single line of Fraunces extra-bold, and treats every section as a printed spread with folio numbers and rule-driven margins. Riskier because some buyers may read it as journalistic rather than advisory.

## Alternate direction B — "Operator's Ledger" (probability ~0.03)

A sparer, ledger-driven approach where the homepage reads as an annual disclosure document. Practice areas are presented as a tabular index with column rules; the practitioner section is laid out like a CV; the training calendar is a literal schedule table. Carries the strongest "institutional" signal but loses some warmth that the editorial direction preserves through photography.

## Why the chosen direction wins

Editorial-with-red gives ABMS three things at once. It avoids the rejected SaaS-template grammar entirely. It allows the existing red logo to remain mandatory while making it look intentional rather than residual. And it lets the homepage and the G7 landing page share a single visual system while serving very different jobs — the homepage stays evergreen and institutional, while the G7 page adds operational urgency without breaking the language.
